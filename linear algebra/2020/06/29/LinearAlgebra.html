Definitions

The two primary mathematical entities that are of interest in linear algebra are the vector and the matrix. They are examples of a more general entity known as a tensor. Tensors possess an order (or rank), which determines the number of dimensions in an array required to represent it. Source:quantstart

Scalars are single numbers and are an example of a 0th-order tensor. $ \to x \in \mathbb{R}$
Vectors are ordered arrays of single numbers and are an example of 1st-order tensor.
Matrices are rectangular arrays consisting of numbers and are an example of 2nd-order tensors.

Vectors

$ x \in \mathbb{R}^n$ define a vector with n elements in the space of real numbers.

By default, vectors are generally represented as columns $x = \left[ \begin{matrix} x_{1}\\ x_{2}\\ \vdots\\x_{n}\\ \end{matrix} \right] $ and the row vector is the transpose $ x^T = \left[ \begin{matrix} x_{1} \ x_{2}\ \cdots \ x_{n}\\ \end{matrix} \right] $

Matrices
